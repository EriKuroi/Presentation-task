Open source failed

“failure” refers not to any technical achievements but rather to the lack of social ones.
The creation of the open-source movement has created a degree of global computing access. To say that open source contributed to the overall innovation of the world would be a shameful understatement. Open source was about the democratization of software and was supposed to smash so-called “digital divide”. That premise was entirely false.
They all built their entire business model on open source software, and they have paid very little in license fees to software vendors. receive the lions’ share of money, forming a part of the 10% of earners leaving everyone beyond.
“Almost all of the increase in income inequality from 1978 to the present can be accounted for by the difference in wages between top performing firms and everyone else.”

Open source software is used by large tech companies to concentrate wealth and power

To be an open source proponent without acknowledging this is to be a very useful fool.

Open source is a future

If we’re able to solve this in terms of open source sustainability, we will have provided a blueprint for solving capitalism’s inequality crisis and saving society itself.
In a 2016 survey from Blackduck, 96% of software products developed that year used open source software. I don’t know how to calculate the total value of open source software to the world, but I do know that if open source software suddenly went away, the results would be catastrophic, an existential crisis for humanity.
